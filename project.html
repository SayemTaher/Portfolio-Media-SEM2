Group Project

Introduction

Requirements: You will develop a brand and website for a real client. Project groups will pitch their ideas to the client, the best work will be selected as their new identity on the web.

Introduction to the Project: During the first week of semester two, we were introduced to our group project, on which we will collaborate for the remainder of the semester. We met our client Erion Kapaj , a Greek artist. During this semester, we will work on creating a professional platform for our client where he can display his artwork and personal life, as well as offer users the option to purchase his work if they so desire. The project requires multiple challenges to overcome as a group, including developing a proper Team Agreement, Project Plan, Survey, Interview, User Research, Prototype , which will lead to implementation , and finally Product Testing . This page will provide a comprehensive overview of our approach to delivering the final product and how I collaborated with my fellow teammates to make it happen.

Step One: Team Agreement

As our first step, we formed a group of six people and named it Homodeus. Even though everyone is committed to actively working together, we wanted to follow certain terms and conditions that were specifically designed for our group. In this regard, we established a group agreement with everyone's consent for each and every rule. We discussed how to avoid risks Â as well as how to deal with or prevent them from occurring. This is one of the first pieces of work we completed for the group project during the first week.

Step Two: Project Plan

Our second task was to develop a proper project plan. It took us some time to research the problem and identify relevant topics that could assist us in completing this project. Here we described the project's goal, problem statement, roles, scope, risk assessment, and current status. The project plan provided us with specific ideas for how we should proceed. We mentioned our research topic and any questions we might have while conducting research on our client. This document provides a clear overview of our project structure.

Step Three: Survey

We had to go through multiple challenges as planned to figure out the majority of the outcome. We need real-life data for this, so we organized our questions and discussed our ideas with our clients, as well as our questions, so that we could offer him the expected outcome as a product. Following that, we launched our Survey for people who are interested in purchasing or learning more about art and artists. This gradually leads us to our user interviews and expert interviews, which will help us create a proper user research based on the data we will receive and then extract the most important thing, which is user requirements.

Task Four: User research

Description: Everything we've learnt thus far from surveys, interviews, and customer requirements is contained in the user research paper. Personas and user scenarios are included. We used all of this data to determine what our users require. Using this paper, we may create a prototype that fits their requirements. Based on what we learn from the user study document, we will use the prototype to finish our product. We'll describe how we got here and what we discovered further down.

Survey Extended

Description: We started our user research process with a survey to determine the general needs and expectations of our target group. To accomplish this, we asked users for permission to complete the survey and share their ideas with us, allowing us to gather information about our users. We printed out the survey QR code and placed it in numerous areas to reach a varied variety of consumers. We opted to publish the QR code in an art gallery here in Eindhoven because we are working for an artist. This enabled us to collect information from people who commonly visit art galleries or attend art exhibitions. We received 20 responses from consumers in all, which provided us with useful insights into their requirements and expectations.

Interview

Description: We performed an expert interview after conducting user interview with a professional artist as part of our user research approach to acquire a better grasp of their needs and expectations when it comes to developing their website. We discovered that the artist desired a visually appealing website that allowed them to present their work in a simple and user-friendly manner. So that construct a website that actually satisfies the demands of artists wishing to explore their online presence by incorporating these insights into our website design process.

Persona

Description: We constructed personas for different age groups after performing user research to better understand their opinions on purchasing art. We were able to uncover common pain areas and sources of inspiration by evaluating the variances and similarities between different age groups. We also included details about their lives and ambitions in our personas to ensure that our design process was based on our users' needs. This will help us to design a prototype that is based on the interests and needs of our target audience by taking these findings into account.

Digital Prototype

Description: After collecting our findings from the user survey, user interviews, and expert interviews, we have compiled all our user research data into a comprehensive set of user requirements. With this information in hand, we began designing a digital prototype for the website's potential user interface. Instead of assigning different pages to each group member, we opted to create individual prototypes for the entire website. This approach allowed us to focus our thought process on design and provide feedback to each other before proceeding to the next steps in creating the final version of our prototype. Below, you'll find two prototypes that my friend and I have created.

Interview

Description: After finalising our prototype, I conducted three separate user interviews to gather feedback on the website's design, layout, and overall user experience. My group members conducted another survey. Based on the findings we have created a storyboard that reflects how things could actually happen in real life , the gestures and expression and how people show body language while providing feedback to certain things. Additionally, I performed a heuristic evaluation to identify any potential usability issues. By collecting this feedback, we were able to pinpoint the necessary changes to improve our portfolio and ensure that the website effectively serves its intended purpose.

Final Prototype

Description: After gathering user feedback, we finalised our prototype with improved functionality and user experience. Throughout the implementation phase, we focused on enhancing performance as much as possible. By analysing user responses, we gained valuable insights that helped us refine the prototype and address any issues. We also incorporated user experience features to make the overall performance better. Our team worked diligently to incorporate user suggestions, optimising the interface for easy navigation and a visually pleasing experience.

Project Report

The project report marks the culmination of our collective work over the past 18 weeks. It serves as a comprehensive documentation, encompassing everything we have accomplished to achieve our desired outcomes. Within its pages, we explain and summarize the tasks we undertook, showcasing how our diligent efforts led us to the expected results. The report also delves into the challenges we encountered along the journey, providing insights into how we tackled and overcame them. Additionally, it emphasizes the invaluable contributions of each team member, highlighting the integral role they played in bringing our project to fruition. Overall, the project report serves as a testament to our dedication, collaboration, and the remarkable progress we have made throughout this endeavor.

Personal Reflection
