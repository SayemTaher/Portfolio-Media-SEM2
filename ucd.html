User Centered Design

Introduction

Requirements: You will dive deep into the world of researching, testing and learning about what your client and users need the most and design your interactive media accordingly.

This page will provide a complete overview on deep user research, critical thinking, research findings , product testing, learning outcomes, feedback & my idiology to excell in building products based on User Centered Design to whatever purpose it may serve.

Task One: Research on Websites

My objective as a User Centered Designer

During my learning period of User-Centered Design, I've realized that user-friendliness and accessibility play a crucial role in developing successful websites. They are essential to ensuring users have a satisfactory experience. As a future user-centered designer, my main priority will be creating products that are easy for users to use and accessible to all. Users are the most important factor in any product, and it is crucial to consider their perspective. If a developer builds a product only from their point of view, users may find it difficult to control or navigate, which can lead to negative outcomes for the company. It can take years to develop a product, but it should be structured to serve the users. Therefore, my main focus will be on maximizing user experience, ensuring maximum accessibility and friendliness, and developing applications that are easy to navigate.

Task: The objective of this assignment was to research and analyze different websites based on user-centered design. The evaluation criteria were based on factors such as accessibility, user experience, design, structure, and navigation, with the aim of assessing whether each website fulfills its intended purpose effectively. The main purpose of the assignment was to identify differences between the websites and understand what makes some websites more user-friendly than others. Ultimately, the goal was to distinguish three websites with good user-centered design from three websites with poor user-centered design.

Three Good UCD

Airbnb: Based on my research, the first website that stands out to me as having good user-centered design is Airbnb's website. The website serves the commercial purpose of allowing users to browse different housing options for temporary residence. I found that the website effectively serves its intended purpose, with the homepage featuring a clear and organized display of various housing options to capture users' attention. The navigation bars are also well-structured and designed for easy user searchability. Overall, I believe that Airbnb's website is an excellent example of user-centered design, and as a user myself, I found it to be a positive user experience.

Apple: Apple Inc. is one of the most recognized companies in the world, and their commitment to serving their users is reflected in the design of their website. Apple invests millions of dollars to ensure that their users have the best possible experience when browsing their products on their website. Their animations and navigation systems are impressive and capable of capturing the attention of almost any customer or potential customer. Apple has been successful in delivering a positive user experience for years, and I believe that their website is an excellent example of effective user-centered design. Therefore, I consider Apple's website to be one of my favorite examples of good user-centered design, as it serves its purpose perfectly in this regard.

Amazon: Amazon's primary objective is to provide the best possible service to their customers and to attract as many customers as possible to their website. Their user-centered design is reflected in their website's user-friendly navigation system, which makes it easy for users to find whatever they are looking for. Additionally, Amazon provides relevant advertisements for other products based on a user's search history, which helps users discover other items that may be of interest to them. The website is also designed to be very user-friendly, with detailed descriptions and reviews for each product, ensuring that users have the best possible experience, even if they ultimately decide not to purchase a product. Overall, I consider Amazon's website to be an excellent example of user-centered design, making it one of my favorite websites.

Three Bad UCD

USPS: The website of The United States Parcel Service is an example of poor user-centered design. The website is cluttered, making it difficult to find information while navigating. As a service system, customers primarily seek to track their orders, yet the website provides only a small input box for entering order details, while focusing on irrelevant content. When accessing the website, it took me some time to locate the tracking feature. Additionally, the navigation portal is overloaded with unnecessary information, which may cause frustration for users seeking specific information. Although the website functions as intended, its poor design detracts from the user experience, making it a negative example of user-centered design.

LinkedIn: While LinkedIn is a widely used social platform for professionals, its user-centered design leaves much to be desired. As an active user, I find it frustrating to change and update my profile information due to the difficulty of locating the relevant options. The platform crams too many features onto a single page, resulting in a cluttered and overwhelming experience for users. Unfortunately, this has become one of my prime examples of a poorly designed application that hinders the user experience.

Yahoo!: Yahoo is one of the oldest computer browser systems and was once widely used by people worldwide. However, while Google has made great strides in creating a clean and user-friendly website, Yahoo has stuck with a cluttered web structure that overwhelms users with too much information. As a user, I struggle to find the information I need in the navigation bar, and the interface lacks a clean and structured design, leading to a poor user experience. As a result, Yahoo remains an example of a platform with inadequate user-centered design.

Learning Outcome: This assignment has led me to think differently about user-centered design. While a website may perform its intended purpose effectively, the user experience can be significantly improved through thoughtful design. As an active web user, I prioritize easy navigation and a clean interface. This assignment has highlighted the importance of user-centered design, which goes beyond serving a website's functional purpose. The research findings have provided key takeaways for me to consider when developing future applications, ultimately leading to better user experiences.

Task Two: Research on COCA-COLA

Description: The goal of this assignment was to thoroughly research Coca Cola's marketing strategy, focusing on how they became the world's most successful soft drink company. We looked at how they executed their strategy to reach every corner of the world, from the richest cities to the most rural villages. Our analysis also included how they've managed to stay ahead of other brands selling similar products. We found that Coca Cola's strategy is based on three main criteria: segmentation, targeting, and positioning, which have been crucial to their ongoing success since the company began.

Segmentation: Coca Cola's segmentation strategy was a game changer for the company, propelling them to success. Instead of limiting their reach to big cities, Coca Cola went to great lengths to distribute their product worldwide. By studying the market of each region, they were able to tailor their product to suit the interests of the local people, which allowed them to dominate the market wherever they went. Additionally, Coca Cola maintained a high standard for their product, setting them apart from competitors. They implemented a balanced marketing strategy that included advertising, using celebrities and campaigns, and even giving away free samples to study the market and customers. Their use of big names in advertising helped to build trust with local people, and their commitment to providing an energy boost while maintaining good health made Coca Cola a popular choice.

Targeting: Coca Cola's customer targeting strategy has been another vital factor in the success of the brand. They have targeted customers ranging from young boys aged 10 to 25 to adults aged 25 to 40, by studying their interests and providing products with different tastes to meet their satisfaction. For customers with heart disease and obesity problems, Coca Cola produced Cola Zero, which contains only a small amount of artificial sugar while providing the same taste but with a milder flavor. In addition to this, Coca Cola introduced other products such as Sprite, which has also gained customer preference. By providing a variety of products and targeting different age groups, Coca Cola ensured that they did not lose their hold on the market.

Positiong: Coca-Cola has implemented an effective multi-segment positioning strategy, positioning itself as the go-to beverage for thirst-quenching and refreshing the body. This is often portrayed in their video advertisements featuring people drinking Coca-Cola and experiencing instant refreshment. Their marketing techniques position Coca-Cola as the drink of choice for fun occasions with friends and family, tired travelers, and people looking to relax on a hot day. Furthermore, Coca-Cola has positioned itself as a premium-quality drink, making it a top choice among consumers. Ask any three people which drink they consider high quality, and Coca-Cola is likely to be the first that comes to mind. This positioning strategy has made Coca-Cola an attractive option for anyone seeking to indulge in life's pleasures.

Learning Outcome: This assignment taught me the value of customer behaviour analysis, market research, effective communication, and marketing innovation. This helped me understand how Coca-Cola created a successful brand that connects with customers and keeps them ahead of the competition in the long run for centuries.

Task Three: Research on The Principles of Psychology in UX

Description: We were assigned to conduct research on psychological aspects of user experience. This includes different ways of structuring the website that provide psychological peace to any user while also making the user experience smooth and accessible. There are a total of nine principles of  psychological aspects in UX that can significantly improve user experience. These include the Principle of Least Effort, Perpetual Habit, Socialisation, Emotional Contagion, Identity, Beauty, The Magical Number Seven, Mistakes and Focus, and Attention & Concentration. Below, I will explain my findings on various websites that correspond to the principles of Psychology in UX.

Least Effort & Beauty: While considering the design aspects of a website, there are numerous websites that can be the perfect match in terms of least effort and beauty, but for me, I discovered that DJI's website appears to be well-designed, organised, and easy to navigate. The navigation bar makes it simple to locate the information they seek.

Perpetual Habit & Magical Number Seven: While talking about Design with the User in Mind As I previously stated, Amazon played a critical role in user-centered design, which always reflected on psychological aspects of users. They included a maximum of seven navigation options, as well as a hamburger menu where users can easily find the information they need.

Principle of Identity: When it comes to knowing a brand or a company solely based on their logo, Apple Inc. has played the most important role in this regard. Regardless of age or gender, almost anyone can recognize an Apple product by its logo. Apple is one of the best examples of identity principles.

Focus, Attention & Concentration: Google Maps was extremely helpful in terms of the Principles of Focus, Attention, and Concentration. And they strive to improve their user experience every year. Google has become one of the most well-known and respected technology companies in the world as a result of their increased concern for their users. Google Maps provides real-time directions and indicators. If a user attempts to travel somewhere, Google Maps provides precise information in terms of station numbers, platform numbers, and current location. This made their applications more user-friendly than any other application that provides the same service.

Principle of Mistakes: It is obvious that human error occurs frequently, but allowing the user to identify and understand the problem and making it simple can improve overall user performance and experience on any website. The Facebook log in section is a good example of UX Mistake Principles.

Emotional Contagion: People frequently refuse to believe in something in the first place if they do not see any proof or statement from others about it. For example, when choosing a good place to eat, people frequently rely on other people's reviews and comments about a particular restaurant's food and services. In this manner, a website can gain the trust of its users as well as potential customers. In terms of Principles of Emotional Contagion, Google Maps did an excellent job. Anyone can easily search anything and find reviews left by people who have used the services.

Principle of Socialization: People frequently follow their favorite websites on social media, especially if they rely on their services. For example, the Dutch Railways' NS portal offers the best service to their customers in terms of purchasing tickets and searching for train schedules. The platform has a lot of information to look through, which can take time away from certain users who are looking for specific information. However, their portal encourages users to contact their social platform, where users can ask and receive answers to their questions most of the time in a very short period of time. Allowing users to connect with social media can improve the user experience of certain platforms.

Learning Outcome: While working on this assignment I learned a lot of aspects how to design and create interfaces that are visually appealing, easy to use, and emotionally engaging for users. This helped me to Understand how users perceive, interpret, allocate their attention, remember, feel. Also understanding what motivates them can help me design interfaces that encourage engagement, interaction, and user satisfaction.
