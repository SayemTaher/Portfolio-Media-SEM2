Prototyping Iteration

Introduction

On this page, I will share my journey of creating a new portfolio for this semester. I will reflect on my thought process and explain how I was able to deliver a portfolio that is different from my previous one.

Thought Process

Since the beginning of this semester, I had a clear vision of doing something different with my portfolio. My main focus was on incorporating animations and enhancing the user experience on my site. A quick comparison with my previous portfolio reveals distinct changes in the header, navigation bar, and overall simplicity of the homepage. For this semester, I dedicated my efforts to improving colour contrast, navigation, and accessibility, aiming to create a pleasant user experience during site exploration. Not only did I want to showcase my work, but I also wanted users to enjoy their time on the site. This led me to develop a completely different footer navigation bar and implement various working and captivating animations, ultimately elevating the overall user experience.

Previous Portfolio

When you explore this site, you will immediately notice the significant changes. The previous portfolio had a deliberate simplicity, designed to serve its purpose effectively. One notable feature of the previous portfolio was its compatibility with various devices, ensuring a seamless user experience. However, with the new portfolio, things have evolved significantly.

New Idea's or inspiration?

The core inspiration for this design came from the layout of Facebook pages. I aimed to create something similar yet unique to my own style. The homepage, in particular, underwent several iterations over a significant period of time, as I continuously made changes to enhance its visual appeal. The addition of an About section and the inclusion of a photography option in the latest iterations do bear some resemblance to a Facebook page, further adding to its uniqueness.

Latest Version

Personally, I find the navigation bar in my current portfolio to be exceptionally cool. Although it might be biased for me to appreciate my own work, I can't help but acknowledge a unique aspect of this navigation bar. Its versatility stands out as a noteworthy feature. I have designed it in such a way that it can be easily modified and adapted for almost any device, which adds a significant level of convenience and flexibility. For the final version of my portfolio I have worked with the fonts. I increased the weight of the font for some cases which significantly enhanced the overall outlook of the pages. For instance the texts on the front page is now catches attention faster also I have replaced the github icon on the header with my brand name that I created for Media Production

Feedback: I received tremendous appreciation for the layout, design, and overall structure of this portfolio, particularly from my UCD, Media Production, and Development teacher. In order to gauge the experience of exploring my portfolio, I sought feedback from both my group mates and friends from other classes. The response was overwhelmingly positive, with the site earning high praise from nearly everyone who visited it.

Learning Outcomes: During this semester I have learned and practiced a lot to enhance the design of my portfolio to try out different things. Yet this will never end here. Even though I believe the latest design will serve it's purpose I hope to work on different other things for instance more interactive yet useful animation implementation . last semester I worked with web responsiveness and this semester I worked with animation and the UI . I am eagerly waiting to develop my next portfolio website where I will use JavaScript and CSS framework to widely chnage the User Interface and User Experience on my site in the upcoming semester. Yes, that's my next objective.
